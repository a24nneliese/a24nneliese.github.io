Anneliese's Pick Facts

Leonardo da Vinci (Art )

1. Leonardo da Vinci secretly dissected over 30 human bodies,

often at night, to understand anatomy for his art, creating remarkably

detailed drawings of muscles, bones, and organs, revealing a mechanical

view of the body and leading to breakthroughs like understanding the heart's

valves, though his findings weren't published for centuries, remaining a secret

masterpiece of Renaissance science.

Phineas Gage (Psychology )

2. Phineas Gage, a 19th-century railroad worker, survived a horrific

accident where a 13-pound iron rod shot through his head, lodging

in his frontal lobe, yet remained conscious, walking and talking shortly

after, becoming a landmark case showing the brain's role in personality,

though reports of his "changed" personality are often exaggerated;

he eventually worked as a stagecoach driver in Chile, demonstrating

neuroplasticity before dying years later from epileptic seizures, his skull

and the rod preserved as scientific artifacts.
